# 3.2. Non-Functional Requirements

Non-functional requirements (NFRs) define the qualities the system must exhibit,
independent of any specific feature. They shaped many architectural and
implementation decisions across the project. The NFRs are grouped into four
areas:

- **[3.2.1. Performance](./nfr_performance.md)** — responsiveness and efficiency.
- **[3.2.2. Scalability & Reliability](./nfr_scalability.md)** — handling growth
  and staying available.
- **[3.2.3. Security](./nfr_security.md)** — protecting users and their data.
- **[3.2.4. Usability & Accessibility](./nfr_usability.md)** — making the
  platform pleasant and usable for everyone.
