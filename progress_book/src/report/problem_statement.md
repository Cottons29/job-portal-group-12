# 2.1. Problem Statement

Students who want part-time work face several overlapping problems:

- **Fragmented discovery.** Listings live in disconnected channels — Facebook
  groups, Telegram chats, word of mouth — making it hard to search, filter, or
  compare opportunities in one place.
- **Lack of trust and verification.** There is no reliable signal that an
  employer or a student is genuine, which exposes both sides to scams and
  no-shows.
- **No application tracking.** Once a student applies, the process becomes a
  black box: there is rarely visibility into whether an application was viewed,
  shortlisted, or rejected.
- **Poor matching.** Generic boards surface roles by recency, not by how well a
  student's skills fit the requirements, so relevant opportunities are buried.
- **Disjointed communication.** Coordination happens off-platform, so context,
  history, and notifications are lost.

For employers, the mirror-image problems exist: low-quality applicant pools, no
structured way to track candidates through stages, and friction in reaching the
right students.

The portal addresses these gaps by combining a structured job marketplace,
identity and verification, skill-based matching, an explicit recruitment
pipeline, and in-app real-time communication.
